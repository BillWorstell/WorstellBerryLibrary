# *Fielding’s Caribbean, 1991* — Margaret Zellers

### Why read this
This 13th edition of *Fielding’s Caribbean* offers a vivid lens on Caribbean travel at the close of the 20th century. Whether you're researching the evolution of tourism, vintage travel styles, or regional presentation, this guidebook provides insights into how each island was framed for visitors—complete with politics, lifestyle, lodging, and even “Treasures and Trifles.”

### What it’s about (concise overview)
A comprehensive, island-by-island handbook for travelers across the Caribbean. Beyond logistics like arrival tips and hotel options, Zellers includes rich thematic entries—natural surroundings, local quirks, medical info, and cultural must-sees. Particularly valuable is its emphasis on “places worth finding,” giving researchers a peek into 1991’s travel priorities.

### Author & perspective
Margaret Zellers blends practical travel advice with editorial personality. Her entries balance facts with subjectivity—pointing out where to splurge, what to skip, and what quirks to expect. The tone is informative yet warm, with traces of the jet-set, mid-century travel era.

### Reception & impact
Popular in its time for its opinionated take and insider advice, *Fielding’s Caribbean* was part of a series that helped define late-20th-century travel writing. Researchers will appreciate the period framing, vocabulary, and selective focus.

### Scope & style
- **Scope:** Caribbean islands across linguistic, political, and geographic divides (Greater & Lesser Antilles, Dutch/French/British territories)  
- **Themes:** Travel logistics, tourist experience, lodging & food, “best of” travel categories, natural/cultural highlights  
- **Style:** Structured but breezy. Uses repeated subcategories across islands (e.g. Political Picture, Lodging, Natural Surroundings)

### Publication & readership
- **Type:** Mass-market travel guide paperback  
- **Audience:** Leisure travelers in the early 1990s, travel agents, researchers of tourism, cultural geographers  
- **Apparatus:** Extensive TOC, summary charts, cross-references between islands
